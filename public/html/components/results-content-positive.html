What does this result mean?

Your family history suggests you may be at a higher risk for having a harmful genetic change in the BRCA1 or BRCA2 genes, which are linked to hereditary breast and ovarian cancer. This means your chances of developing these cancers may be higher than the general population.

What should you do next?

National cancer organizations recommend that women with a positive family history screening meet with a genetic counselor. Genetic counselor will:

  • Gather more detailed information about your family's cancer history
  • Assess the likelihood of an inherited cause for cancer in your family
  • Explain the benefits and limitations of genetic testing
  • Discuss your personal cancer risk and explore ways to lower it
  • Review insurance coverage and financial assistance programs

Genetic counseling helps you make an informed decision about whether to pursue genetic testing. Genetic testing looks at your saliva or blood to check for gene changes that may increase your risk for inherited cancers.
