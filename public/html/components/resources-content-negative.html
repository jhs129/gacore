Take action for your health

Mammogram screening

Regular check-ups can help find breast cancer early, when it's easier to treat. If you're 40 or older, healthcare providers suggest getting a mammogram every two years.

Keep your family history updated

Your family's health history can change as your family members learn new things about their health. Review your family's cancer history every year and share any updates with your provider. These changes could affect your risk.

Maintain a healthy lifestyle

A healthy lifestyle can support your well-being and may reduce certain cancer risks. Here are some key habits and helpful resources to support your journey.

Limit alcohol use

Drinking beer, wine, or liquor, increases your risk of breast cancer. Providers suggest women limit alcohol to one drink per day.

Maintain a healthy weight

Maintain a healthy weight. Ask your provider what's a healthy weight for you.

Eat a balanced diet

A diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins can help support your immune system and reduce cancer risk.

  • MyPlate by the USDA: Use this tool to build a balanced diet tailored to your needs, with meal plans, recipes, and nutritional guidance.
  • American Cancer Society Healthy Eating Guidelines: The ACS provides easy-to-follow dietary recommendations to help reduce cancer risk.
